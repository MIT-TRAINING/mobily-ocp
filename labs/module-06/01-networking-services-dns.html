One request, four networks.

A subscriber hits the self-care portal. That request crosses four distinct networks before it reaches a pod. Trace it, watch CoreDNS resolve a Service, and see ClusterIP load-balance across healthy endpoints.

Four address spaces: external (public DNS/LB), node (the RHCOS hosts + router), service (virtual ClusterIPs), pod (every pod's IP). Most connectivity bugs are "which network is the break on?"

DNS resolution

Pick where the caller lives. In the same project you can use a short name; across projects you need the namespace.

$ nslookup subscriber-api …
Every Service gets name.namespace.svc.cluster.local. CoreDNS returns the Service's ClusterIP, not a pod IP — the ClusterIP is stable even as pods churn.

ClusterIP load-balancing across endpoints

The Service fronts 3 pods. Click a pod to toggle its health — unhealthy pods leave the EndpointSlice and stop getting traffic. Then send traffic.

healthy endpoints: 3/3 requests sent: 0
Only Ready pods are in the EndpointSlice. The Service spreads requests across them — kill one and traffic rebalances to the survivors automatically.
