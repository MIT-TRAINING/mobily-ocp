Concept · Firewalling the pod network

Open by default. Locked by policy.

The pod network starts wide open — any pod can reach any pod, even across tenants. NetworkPolicies are additive allow-only rules that fix that. Toggle policies for team-billing and watch the connection verdicts — and cross-tenant isolation — change live.

Policies in project team-billing

Tick to apply. Remember: a pod with no policy is fully open; the moment a default-deny selects it, only explicitly-allowed traffic gets through.

With no policies, everything is allowed — including team-crm reaching the billing database. Not what you want on a shared cluster.

The multi-tenancy recipe

Network isolation is one layer; a real tenant boundary stacks several.

Per-project baseline: default-deny-ingress + allow-same-namespace + allow-from-ingress. New projects can get these automatically via a project template.
Full stack: Project + RBAC (who) + ResourceQuota/LimitRange (how much) + NetworkPolicy (who talks to whom) + route sharding (how exposed). Isolate by default, share by exception.
