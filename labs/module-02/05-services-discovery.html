Concept · Service discovery, labels & DNS

Pods churn. The Service stays put.

Pods are born and die with fresh IPs all day long — but callers never chase those IPs. They use one stable name. Toggle pod labels and health below and watch the Service's endpoints and load-balancing recompute live.

The subscriber-api Service

subscriber-api.selfcare.svc.cluster.local
ClusterIP 172.30.42.10 : 8080  ·  stable, never changes
selector: app=subscriber-api

A Service does not list pod IPs. It declares a selector. The endpoints controller continuously watches for pods that (1) match the selector and (2) are Ready, and keeps the endpoint list in sync. Change anything below and watch it react.

The pods (your fleet)

Click a pod's label to flip it in/out of the selector. Toggle Ready to simulate a failing health probe. A pod is an endpoint only when it both matches and is Ready.

Live endpoints behind the Service

Requests hit the ClusterIP and kube-proxy load-balances them across the current endpoints…

The one takeaway

You never typed a pod IP. Callers resolve subscriber-api via DNS to one stable ClusterIP; labels + selectors decide membership and readiness decides eligibility, so the endpoint set self-adjusts as pods come, go, and fail. That decoupling is exactly why a dynamic, self-healing cluster stays usable.
