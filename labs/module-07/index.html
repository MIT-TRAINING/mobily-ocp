OpenShift storage & security — visualized

Three hands-on visualizations that make Module 7 click — how a PVC binds to a PV that a StorageClass provisions on demand (and how data survives a pod restart), how Security Context Constraints keep pods from running as root, and how ServiceAccounts and Secrets give a pod a safe identity and its credentials. Each ends with a short self-check quiz.

How to use this

Work through the three in order (each builds on the last) or jump to any concept. Everything runs in your browser — no install, no internet, no cluster needed. Companion reading: guides/module-07-storage-security.md. Then practice for real with the guided demos and exercises using oc on the shared OpenShift 4.18 cluster.

What you'll be able to explain afterwards

  • How a PVC → PV binds, how a StorageClass dynamically provisions, and why data survives a pod restart
  • Access modes (RWO vs RWX) and reclaim policy (Delete vs Retain) — and where ODF fits
  • Why restricted-v2 refuses root and assigns a random UID — and why rootless UBI images "just work"
  • How to loosen security correctly: grant a ServiceAccount a broader SCC, never edit the pod
  • How ServiceAccounts give pods identity and Secrets deliver credentials (and why base64 ≠ encryption)
