Concept · Durable data

Pods are disposable. Your data shouldn't be.

A PVC is a request for storage; a StorageClass provisions a real PV on demand and binds it. Mount it in a pod and the data outlives the pod. Provision the CDR archive, then kill the pod and watch the data survive.

Dynamic provisioning: PVC → StorageClass → PV → Bound

Create the claim and watch the platform make a disk and bind it — no admin ticket.

You write only the PVC (size + access mode). The StorageClass's CSI provisioner creates a real disk, wraps it as a PV, and binds it 1:1 to your PVC — status goes Pending → Bound.

Data survives the pod

Mount the bound PVC at /data, write a CDR, then delete the pod. A replacement re-attaches the same volume.

📦 cdr-writer pod mounts
💾/data — (empty)
The volume has its own lifecycle. Delete the pod and the disk (and its data) remain; a new pod mounting the same PVC sees the CDRs intact. That's the whole point of persistence.
