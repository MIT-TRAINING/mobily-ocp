Concept · Backup & Recovery

A snapshot is a rewind point. Restore rewinds everything.

Take an etcd snapshot, then keep creating objects. Click masters to fail them and watch quorum. When disaster strikes, the number of masters down decides your path: heal, replace a member, or the break-glass restore — which rewinds the whole cluster to the snapshot and loses everything created since. That gap is your RPO.

Take a snapshot, create a few objects, then fail masters and hit Disaster → recover. The recovery path depends on how many masters are down.

Restore vs. replace — the decision that matters most

0 down → healthy, nothing to do.   1 down (quorum 2/3 holds) → replace the etcd member — no data loss, no rewind.   2–3 down (quorum lost) → restore from snapshot on one master — the cluster rewinds and every object created since the snapshot is gone.
cluster-backup.sh takes the snapshot (copy it off the cluster!). cluster-restore.sh replays it as a new single-member etcd, the others rejoin. Fresh snapshots = smaller RPO = less lost on restore.
