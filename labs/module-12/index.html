Then practice for real with the guided demos and exercises (backup/restore & node ops need cluster-admin; the capstone is mostly project-user).

What you'll be able to explain afterwards

  • How etcd backup (cluster-backup.sh) and restore work — and why restore is a break-glass, whole-cluster rewind you copy off-cluster and take before risky changes
  • The restore-vs-replace decision: lose one master → replace the member; lose quorum → restore. Why etcd ≠ app-data backup (PV data needs OADP; config needs Git)
  • The node lifecycle — cordon → drain (honouring PDBs) → delete → replace — and what control-plane replacement adds
  • A repeatable troubleshooting methodology: narrow by layer (platform via oc get co/nodes/etcd vs. app via pod status), let events narrate, then read logs
  • How every layer of the course composes into one end-to-end service — Route, Service, Deployment, Secret, PVC, NetworkPolicy, monitoring — delivered by GitOps and made recoverable by backup & troubleshooting
