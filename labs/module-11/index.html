Monitoring, Logging & etcd — visualized

Three hands-on visualizations that make Module 11 click — how Prometheus turns a metric into an alert (and how for: filters blips before Alertmanager pages anyone), how Loki / LogQL narrows a flood of logs to the one line that matters, and how etcd stays alive on quorum — and why a fast disk is non-negotiable. Each ends with a short self-check quiz.

How to use this

Work through the three in order (they trace an incident from alert → logs → ruling out etcd) or jump to any concept. Everything runs in your browser — no install, no internet, no cluster needed. Companion reading: guides/module-11-monitoring-logging-etcd.md. Then practice for real with the guided demos and exercises (the monitoring stack is built-in; logging is an admin-installed Operator; etcd checks run from the control plane).

What you'll be able to explain afterwards

  • The built-in monitoring stack (Cluster Monitoring Operator, Prometheus, Alertmanager, Thanos) and the metrics/logs division of labour
  • How an alerting rule works — expression + for:, Pending vs Firing — and how Alertmanager groups, routes and silences
  • Why your apps need user workload monitoring + a ServiceMonitor before you can alert on them
  • The logging pipeline collect → store → visualize (Vector → LokiStack → LogQL), the three log types, and how to filter efficiently
  • Why etcd quorum is life-or-death, why masters are odd-numbered, and which health & performance signals (fsync, leader changes) to watch
