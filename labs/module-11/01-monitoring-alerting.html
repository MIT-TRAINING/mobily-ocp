Concept · Monitoring & Alerting

A metric crosses a line. An alert wakes someone up.

Prometheus scrapes subscriber-api's 5xx error rate. An alerting rule says "fire if it stays above 5% for 10 minutes." Drag the error rate, watch the rule move Inactive → Pending → Firing, and see Alertmanager route it by severity. The for: window is what turns a blip into a page — or doesn't.

Below 5%, the rule's expression is false — nothing fires. Push the rate above the threshold and hold it: the rule goes Pending, and only after the full for: 10m window does it become Firing and reach Alertmanager. (The demo compresses 10 minutes into a few seconds.)

The PrometheusRule behind it

kind: PrometheusRule
  alert: SubscriberAPIHighErrorRate
  expr: sum(rate(http_requests_total{code=~"5.."}[5m])) / sum(rate(http_requests_total[5m])) > 0.05
  for: 10m
  labels: { severity: warning }
Prometheus evaluates expr every interval; for: filters blips; the severity label tells Alertmanager where to route. Prometheus fires; Alertmanager groups, routes, silences.
